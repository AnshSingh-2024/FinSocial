Community Driven Brokerage
Platform
FinTech / Community Platform / Full Stack Engineering

TRACK FinTech / Community Platform / Full Stack Engineering TEAM SIZE 4 Members

Background
Retail investors do not trade in isolation — they follow trends, ask questions, and take cues from
peers. Yet every brokerage app treats users as individuals in silos. The opportunity is a
community-first layer on top of a brokerage platform — where collective intelligence becomes a
feature, not a side effect.

Problem Statement
Build a community-driven simulated brokerage platform available for web and mobile. The
platform should let users connect with fellow investors — from complete beginners to
experienced traders — discover what peers are trading, exchange signals and insights, get help
with platform and investing basics, and participate in community-driven discussions. The goal is
to make investing less intimidating and more social, turning collective knowledge into a financial
edge for everyone.


SCENARIO
A first-time investor signs up with no idea where to begin. They head to the Beginner's
Lounge Tribe Room and ask "How do I read a candlestick chart?" — an experienced
trader replies within minutes. Feeling confident, they browse the Community Feed,
check the Signal Board, and place their first virtual trade. All in one place, on any
device.

Objectives
— Welcome beginners with a community that helps them learn and get started
— Show anonymized peer trading activity and community trends
— Surface crowd-sourced and algorithm-assisted BUY / SELL signals
— Enable community Q&A on investing basics, platform help, and stock discussions
— Provide topic-based discussion rooms for focused conversations
— Track community sentiment per stock or sector
— Support a simulated brokerage experience with historical price replay
— Implement secure JWT-based authentication and responsive design

What to Build
Community Feed
A real-time-style feed showing anonymized peer activity — what stocks are being bought or sold
across the platform, trending tickers, and notable portfolio moves. Users can react, comment, or
follow a trend directly from the feed.

Signal Board
BUY and SELL signals surfaced from community consensus (upvoted signals) and simple
algorithmic indicators on historical OHLCV data. Each signal shows the stock, type, confidence
level, and plain-English reasoning.


Tribe Rooms
Topic-based community discussion rooms:
· Beginner's Lounge — ask any question, no matter how basic
· IPO Watch — discuss upcoming IPOs
· Sector Spotlight — IT, Pharma, Banking and beyond
· Platform Help — understand features and navigate portfolios
· Mutual Funds Corner — SIPs, index funds, and long-term investing

Q&A Forum
A structured peer Q&A board where users post investment-related questions and receive
community responses ranked by helpfulness — Stack Overflow for investors.

Sentiment Meter
A per-stock community sentiment indicator — Bullish / Neutral / Bearish — derived from
aggregated signals, reactions, and discussion tone.

Virtual Portfolio & Brokerage Simulation
Users place virtual BUY and SELL trades driven by historical price replay. Buy price is taken from
the historical dataset; P&L = (latest price − buy price) × quantity.


Feature Expectations
Must-Have
— User registration and JWT-based secure login
— Community feed showing anonymised peer trading activity and trending stocks
— Signal Board with BUY / SELL signals, confidence level, and reasoning
— At least three Tribe Rooms including Beginner's Lounge and Platform Help
— Q&A forum with upvote and reply functionality
— Virtual portfolio with buy, sell, and P&L tracking
— Fully responsive UI for desktop and mobile

Good to Have (optional)
— Leaderboard showing top-performing virtual portfolios
— AI-assisted reply suggestions in Q&A using financial context
— Push-style notifications for signals and Q&A responses
— Verified experienced trader badges for community-recognised mentors

Dataset Guidance
Use publicly available historical OHLCV stock data. Suggested sources: NSE/BSE historical
exports, Kaggle, Yahoo Finance, or AI-generated datasets.
— 20+ stocks with at least 2 years of daily closing price data
— Powers both the Signal Board and Virtual Portfolio replay
— Seed: 5–8 dummy accounts with pre-populated trades and posts
— Seed: Tribe Rooms with starter discussions and beginner questions
